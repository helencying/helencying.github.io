# Yoga class

July 5, 2026

Today, I went to acac Fitness & Wellness in West Broad Village to take a yoga class with Zi and Steven. We waited a bit at the front desk to 
get checked in by the manager since they were stuck on the phone for quite some time. We were a little worried we wouldn't make it to the 
9:30AM yoga class on time so we asked if we could just go to the yoga class first and then get checked in after, but the other available
non-manager front desk worker said they weren't allowed to let me up without checking me in. Thankfully, we still made it to the yoga class
on time but the only available spot was the space right in front of the instructor. I didn't mind and enjoyed being at the front and
being able to see the instructor clearly, though some of the other folks in the room called us "brave souls" or something like that. I thought
that was quite funny. 

The class started with some Taichi-like movements, going from side to side, slight bend, pushing and moving with our arms. Then we flowed into downward
dog, upward dog, warrior 1, warrior 2, and so forth. The hardest and most challenging poses were the chair poses on our toes, that was really
burning my quads, as well as some of the more balancing poses. Overall, I had a fun time at the class, it was challenging, relaxing, and a good
mix of stretches. I could still feel the tightness in my left hamstring though. I need to look up some ways to reduce and relieve the tightness there. 

After the yoga class, Zi and Steven gave me a tour of acac. I think this was my first time in a really legit gym with classes as well as free weights, machines,
barbells and so forth. I guess this is what gyms in the suburbs are like? Or maybe what fancier gyms are like. I think if I were to move back to NYC, 
I may want to splurge a little and get a membership to a nice gym like this as well. It would be great to alternate between taking classes, doing
weight training on my own, and so much more. I think I am still happy with my apartment gym for now though but it does get a little lonely and going
to a workout class with other people definitely increases motivation and enjoyment. 

Reflections after the yoga class
- I wonder if I should try to get a yoga certification and become a yoga instructor? Or at least have the ability to teach yoga. I've been doing yoga since
2020 and it is really enjoyable and fun. Getting certified would definitely give me another avenue of income if I wanted to. I also thought the yoga
instructor today was very cool and she was teaching another class immediately after the yoga class. I did some research on becoming yoga certified afterwards but it sounds like it isn't exactly a lucrative financial path. Nonetheless, I do think being able to teach yoga may be fun perhaps? Or maybe yoga and doing yoga with other people is something I would enjoy more. This is something I can look into when I am older. https://www.reddit.com/r/yoga/comments/10by7hl/considering_becoming_a_yoga_teacher_need_input/
- Signing up for a gym membership is something I want to try out eventually. Regardless of the money, I think it is a positive investment
in my physical health and exercise in general is what I want to continue doing everyday.
